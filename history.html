Our story

From humble beginnings to a regional centre of excellence

PNGC IHE’s legacy is tied to Catholic missionaries, Port Moresby community leaders and pioneering lecturers who believed every village deserves highly trained teachers, health workers and administrators.

Read our chronicle

Historical timeline

1978 – Consultation phase

Catholic Archdiocese of Port Moresby, National Capital District Commission and Caritas Australia conducted feasibility studies to establish a Catholic tertiary college.

1981 – Opening cohort

First 32 students enrolled in certificate programs for teaching and parish administration under the leadership of Sr. Benigna Maria.

1990s – Regional reach

Mobile training teams brought short courses to remote districts including Kompiam, Kandep and Lagaip.

2008 – Accreditation milestone

DHERST granted full registration, allowing PNGC IHE to award diplomas recognised across PNG and the Pacific.

2020 – Digital pivot

When the pandemic disrupted travel, faculty launched Moodle classrooms, radio lessons and WhatsApp tutoring to keep students learning.

Legacy projects

Library of voices

Digitising oral histories from elders and clergy to preserve Port Moresby's knowledge systems for future learners.

Women of courage

Scholarships honouring the first female graduates who now lead diocesan education offices and NGOs.

Community schools renewal

Alumni partner with parish committees to renovate and reopen rural schools closed during tribal conflicts.

Highlands research archives

Collection of theses, maps and ecological studies accessible to local government planners.
